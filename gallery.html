Gallery of St. Nikolaus Church and Tower

Browse highlights from the church, tower, and panoramic views over Rosenheim.

Exterior view of St. Nikolaus Church Tower

Tower captured from ground floor.

St. Nikolaus Church Building

The front side of the St. Nikolaus Church.

The Baroque tower dome a.k.a Zwiebelhelm

Zoomed view of the dome.

Wooden Stairs to climb the tower

One of the many stairs to climb the tower. With these stairs, the visitors can climb their way to the top.

Baselbalg

The wooden blower for the church organ. It supplies the necessary air for the organ to produce music.

Dachboden or the church attic

Beautiful example of wood-engineering. Fully made of wood, without using much iron.

Stone engraving at the Attic

Written in middle-old German, this old engraving depicts the chronological history of the church and the tower.

A very old door

This cast iron door is from around 1300s. It is one of the oldest objects in the tower.

Old and renovated wooden stairs

This stair leads to the attic area.

Tower window

One of the many windows, showing beautiful town of Rosenheim.

View from top

A view from height equal to to a 10 story building, one of the major attractions, provides a magnificent top view of the city of Rosenheim.

Beautiful Top view of Rosenheim

The top of the tower is surrounded with nets to ensure protection from birds and unwanted accidents.

The Dreifaltigkeitsglocke or the Trinity Bell.

The Largest Bell of the tower, with a weight more than 3 tonnes. Build by Czudnochowsky, Erding, this bell delivers the deepest sound.

The Wooden Bell frame

Wooden frame that provided necessary flexibility and protects the tower.

The Christusglocke

The second largest bell, named after Lord Jesus Christ.
